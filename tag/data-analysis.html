Project Background¶

The Millennium Development Goals are eight goals that all 191 UN member states have agreed to try to achieve by the year 2015:

  • to eradicate extreme poverty and hunger
  • to achieve universal primary education
  • to promote gender equality and empower women
  • to reduce child mortality
  • to improve maternal health
  • to combat HIV/AIDS, malaria, and other diseases
  • to ensure environmental sustainability
  • to develop a global partnership for development

Data Source¶

The data is collected from Open Development Website, an open data aggregation and sharing network and can be accessed here. It was disclosed on the web page that Open Development organization extracted this data from Asian Development Bank's Statistical Database System (SDBS).

The CSV file includes measurements, for countries in the lower mekong basin, which are Myanmar, Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia and Laos, of five indicators of Millennium Development goals :

  • Proportion of population using improved drinking water sources
  • Proportion of population using improved sanitation facilities
  • Telephone subscribers per 100 population
  • Cellular subscribers per 100 population
  • Internet Users per 100 Population for countries in the lower mekong basin, Myanmar, Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia and Laos

Please note that the data used in this notebook was slightly different from the online data set due to some minor data cleaning.

We'll visualize the indicator trends for each category of goal measurement.

Assuming, telephone subscription strictly means landline telephone subscription excluding mobile cellphones, telephone subscriber is a different kind of indicator than the two above (percent with access to improved water sources and sanitation conditions). It might have been a great indicator to measure the development of a country, back when the project was announced at Millennium Summit in 2000. Mobile cell phone subscription has taken over the landline subscription since then, and unsurprisingly, we can see that in all five countries, except Vietnam, the telephone subscription per 100 population has plateaued.

We can see that for all countries except Myanmar, up to the latest year in data which is 2013, cellular usage has been growing at an exponential rate, with the user count per 100 going above 100 for Vietnam, Cambodia and Thailand. Having values above 100 for user count(per 100) may be explained by overcounting of the population of users who carry multiple SIM cards and hence being counted multiple times.

For Myanmar, we will need more data for years after 2013 to verify its exponential rate of growth for mobile phone usage.
